Five Pillars of Islam

1. Faith

This is the declaration of faith and the first pillar of Islam. It involves bearing witness and openly proclaiming the belief in the oneness of Allah and the prophethood of Muhammad. The Shahada is often recited as follows: "La ilaha illallah, Muhammadur rasulullah," which means "There is no god but Allah, and Muhammad is the messenger of Allah."

2. Prayer

Muslims are required to perform five daily prayers facing the Kaaba in Mecca. These prayers are known as Salat and are performed at specific times throughout the day and night. The five daily prayers are Fajr (before sunrise), Dhuhr (midday), Asr (afternoon), Maghrib (just after sunset), and Isha (night).

3. Fasting

Fasting during the holy month of Ramadan is the fourth pillar. Muslims fast from dawn (Fajr) until sunset (Maghrib) throughout the month of Ramadan. Fasting involves abstaining from food, drink, and other physical needs during daylight hours. It is a spiritual practice aimed at self-discipline, self-purification, and empathy for the less fortunate.

4. Zakat

Zakat is the obligation to give a portion of one's wealth to those in need, typically 2.5% of one's savings and investments, after the deduction of necessary expenses. It is a form of almsgiving and is intended to help the poor and needy.

5. Hajj

Hajj is the fifth pillar and is a pilgrimage to the holy city of Mecca in Saudi Arabia. It is obligatory for every Muslim who is physically and financially able to undertake the journey at least once in their lifetime. Hajj occurs annually during the Islamic month of Dhu al-Hijjah and involves a series of rituals, including circumambulating the Kaaba, standing at the plain of Arafat, and other acts of worship.
